The Legal Status of Bitcoin in India

In India, the regulatory environment for Bitcoin and other cryptocurrencies has been a subject of evolving debate and development. As of now, Bitcoin is not illegal in India, but it is not recognized as legal tender either. The Indian government has not banned cryptocurrencies, but it has imposed certain regulations to monitor their use.

In 2018, the Reserve Bank of India (RBI) issued a circular prohibiting banks from facilitating transactions involving cryptocurrencies. This circular was subsequently challenged in the Supreme Court of India, and in March 2020, the court overturned the RBI's ban, allowing banks to facilitate cryptocurrency transactions once again.
